How the WOD Generator Works

        
Select a workout format (AMRAP, EMOM, For Time, or Chipper), pick your equipment, and choose RX, Scaled, or Beginner difficulty. The generator randomly combines movements from our curated pool — each format follows standard CrossFit time domains and rep schemes used in affiliate and garage-gym training.


        

          Expert Note
          
Random WODs are useful for variety days, but they are not a replacement for a structured program. Use generated workouts on active-recovery or skill days, and follow periodized plans when preparing for a race or competition.

        


        
Workout Formats Explained

        
AMRAP (As Many Rounds As Possible): Fixed time cap, maximize rounds. Teaches pacing under fatigue.

        
EMOM (Every Minute On the Minute): Work starts each minute; leftover time is rest. Builds consistent output.

        
For Time: Complete prescribed rounds as fast as possible. Tests sustained power.

        
Chipper: Long single-round list of movements. Rewards smart partitioning and grip management.


        
Safety & Scaling

        
Always warm up before high-intensity work. Scale load and gymnastics progressions to your current capacity. Stop if you feel sharp pain, dizziness, or chest discomfort. Generated workouts are educational templates — not personalized coaching. See our Terms of Service for the full disclaimer.

Building a Weekly Mix

        
A practical week for mixed-modal athletes: one heavy strength day, one generated AMRAP/EMOM for conditioning, one aerobic easy run (zone 2 via Zone 2 Calculator), one rest day. Random WODs fill the conditioning slot when you lack programming energy — not every slot in the week.
