How It Works

The calculator supports four Zone 2 approaches: age-based, known max-HR, Karvonen reserve, and LTHR-based. For runners with threshold data, the LTHR option uses a practical Zone 2 range around 85-89% of threshold heart rate, which usually aligns better with sustainable aerobic work than generic formulas.

If Garmin Zone 2 feels too low for your easy long runs, that is common when device defaults are anchored to broad population equations. Switching to LTHR or Karvonen often produces a range that matches your breathing and decoupling data more closely.

Expert Note

A good Zone 2 target should let you speak in short sentences, keep cadence relaxed, and finish with stable form. If pace fades sharply at the same heart rate, your true ceiling is probably 2-4 bpm lower than the formula output.

Accuracy & Limitations

No single equation is perfect because hydration, temperature, terrain, and stress can shift heart rate on a given day. Wrist optical sensors from Garmin or COROS can also read low or high during cold starts and fast arm-swing changes.

Use this as a starting range, then validate with your long easy sessions and adjust if your effort consistently feels too easy or too taxing. For full-zone planning, pair this with the Heart Rate Zones Calculator.

Real-World Examples

A runner using age-based Zone 2 at 118-131 bpm felt underloaded and saw no aerobic progress. After testing LTHR at 170 bpm, their 85-89% range shifted to 145-151 bpm, making long runs productive while still conversational.

Another athlete using Karvonen with max 192 and resting 52 got a realistic Zone 2 band for trail days, then lowered the cap by 3 bpm in summer heat to maintain the same effort quality.

FAQ

Why does Garmin Zone 2 feel easier than this calculator?

Garmin often starts with generalized zone presets unless you manually set max HR or threshold values. Personalized methods like LTHR and Karvonen usually track experienced runners better.

When should I use the LTHR method?

Use it when you have a reliable threshold heart-rate value from a recent test or race effort. It is typically the most specific method for structured run training.

Should Zone 2 stay the same all year?

No. Recheck every training block or after major fitness changes, and adjust for climate or fatigue so easy days remain truly aerobic.
