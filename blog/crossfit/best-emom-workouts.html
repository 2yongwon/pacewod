Best EMOM Workouts for Every Level

Updated July 2026 · 11 min read

EMOM — Every Minute On the Minute — is one of the most versatile workout formats in functional fitness. You perform a set of work at the start of each minute and rest for whatever time remains. Simple structure, brutal effectiveness.

Need a random workout right now? Try the WOD Generator and select EMOM format.

How EMOM Works

At the top of each minute, complete the prescribed reps. If you finish in 30 seconds, you rest 30 seconds. If you finish in 50 seconds, you rest 10. The clock keeps you honest — slow down and your rest disappears.

Why EMOM Trains Engine Differently Than AMRAP

In an AMRAP, athletes self-select rest — fitter athletes hide inefficiency by shortening breaks. EMOM removes that option. Work is fixed to the clock; rest is whatever remains. The format forces sustainable power output across 10–30 minutes instead of a single 6-minute redline.

Pacing math: In a 10-minute EMOM with 10 air squats per minute, finishing squats in 22 seconds leaves 38 seconds rest (63% recovery). By minute 8, if squats slow to 35 seconds, rest drops to 25 seconds (42% recovery) — HR climbs, form degrades, and the session becomes metabolic. Target 15–25 seconds rest on minute 1; if rest falls below 10 seconds by minute 5, reps were set too high.

Expert Note

EMOM is not max-effort every minute. The best EMOM sessions feel almost too easy in round 1 and barely manageable in the final round. If minute 1 requires a gasping effort, reduce reps 20–30% before starting — the clock will still find your limit by minute 8.

Heart Rate and Wearables During EMOM

Wrist HR lags badly on mixed-modal work — burpees spike HR 10–15 bpm late, rowing understates effort during the pull. For repeatable EMOM benchmarking, note average HR from a chest strap on the same workout monthly. Comparing "Engine Builder" EMOM 20 HR month over month at identical rep schemes shows conditioning progress better than subjective RPE alone.

Garmin and COROS Training Effect scores after EMOM often read "Anaerobic" even when average HR sits in zone 3 — the intermittent spikes trigger algorithm flags. Use session RPE (1–10) and rest-interval duration as primary progress markers; treat watch labels as directional.

Beginner EMOMs

Start with 10-minute EMOMs using bodyweight movements. Focus on consistent pacing — you should finish each minute with at least 15 seconds of rest.

EMOM 10 — Bodyweight Basics

  • Min 110 Air Squats
  • Min 28 Push-ups
  • Min 312 Sit-ups
  • Min 48 Lunges (each leg)
  • Min 510 Mountain Climbers (each leg)

Repeat for 2 rounds (10 min total)

Scaling example: If push-ups exceed 40 seconds in minute 2, drop to 5 reps or elevate hands on a box. Maintain the 15-second rest floor — do not rush through degraded push-ups to "finish" the template as written.

EMOM 12 — Cardio + Core

  • Min 115 Single Unders
  • Min 210 Air Squats
  • Min 330 sec Plank Hold
  • Min 48 Burpees

Repeat for 3 rounds (12 min total)

Intermediate EMOMs

Add load and complexity. Aim for 20–24 minute sessions with mixed modalities.

Modality Pairing Logic

Effective EMOM design alternates local muscle stress. Rowing (legs + back) followed by box jumps (legs) accumulates quad fatigue faster than rowing followed by push press (shoulders). The templates below alternate upper, lower, and full-body cardio to distribute load. When building custom EMOMs, avoid back-to-back grip-intensive movements (pull-ups then kettlebell swings) unless grip endurance is the explicit target.

EMOM 20 — The Engine Builder

  • Min 112 KB Swings (24/16 kg)
  • Min 210 Box Jumps (24/20")
  • Min 38 DB Push Press (each arm)
  • Min 415/12 Cal Row
  • Min 510 Burpees

Repeat for 4 rounds (20 min total)

EMOM 24 — Barbell Complex Lite

  • Min 15 Power Cleans (95/65 lb)
  • Min 28 Pull-ups
  • Min 310 Wall Balls (20/14 lb)
  • Min 412/10 Cal Assault Bike
  • Min 56 Front Squats (95/65 lb)
  • Min 620 Double Unders

Repeat for 4 rounds (24 min total)

Advanced EMOMs

These sessions test your ability to maintain output under fatigue. Expect minimal rest in later rounds.

When to Use Advanced Templates

Reserve 24–30 minute loaded EMOMs for athletes who can complete the intermediate "Engine Builder" with 12+ seconds rest on every minute of round 4. Advanced sessions assume proficiency in barbell cycling under fatigue — not learning days. Schedule them 48+ hours after heavy squatting and 24+ hours before a long run if you combine CrossFit with endurance training.

Expert Note

Assault Bike and Ski Erg calories are not interchangeable across machines — 12 cal on an Assault Bike ≈ 15–18 sec of work for most athletes; 12 cal on a Ski Erg ≈ 25–30 sec. When substituting equipment, match work duration, not calorie number. Use a stopwatch on minute 1 to calibrate.

EMOM 30 — Death by Barbell

  • Min 13 Squat Cleans (135/95 lb)
  • Min 25 Chest-to-Bar Pull-ups
  • Min 37 Toes-to-Bar
  • Min 49/7 Cal Ski Erg
  • Min 511 Deadlifts (135/95 lb)

Repeat for 6 rounds (30 min total)

EMOM 20 — Sprint & Grind

  • Min 115/12 Cal Assault Bike (sprint)
  • Min 210 Thrusters (95/65 lb)
  • Min 315/12 Cal Row (sprint)
  • Min 48 Bar Muscle-ups

Repeat for 5 rounds (20 min total)

EMOM Tips

  • Pace the first round. If minute 1 feels easy, you're at the right intensity.
  • Scale reps, not rest. Reduce reps to keep 10–20 seconds of rest rather than rushing through.
  • Track your splits. Note when rest drops below 10 seconds — that's your limit for sustainable EMOM work.
  • Pair complementary movements. Alternate upper and lower body to manage local fatigue.

Progression Framework

Log three variables after every EMOM: slowest minute completion time, average rest per round, and session RPE. Progress when all three stabilize for two consecutive sessions at the same rep scheme:

  1. Increase reps 1–2 per minute while keeping rest above 10 seconds
  2. Add one round (extend 20 min → 24 min) before adding load
  3. Increase load 5–10% only after duration and reps are sustainable

Example: "Engine Builder" at 12 KB swings becomes 14 swings for 4 sessions, then a 5th round, then 28/20 kg instead of 24/16 kg. Jumping straight to heavier load without pacing data produces one heroic session and three weeks of soreness — not adaptation.

EMOM for Runners

Runners using EMOM for cross-training should bias toward low-impact cardio (row, bike, ski) plus core — not heavy eccentric leg work within 48 hours of a long run. A 16-minute EMOM of 12/10 cal row + 30 sec plank + 10 push-ups + 8 goblet squats (light) maintains engine without the DOMS that 50 weighted lunges would trigger before Saturday's long run.

FAQ

How long should an EMOM be for fat loss vs conditioning?

Conditioning adaptation: 16–24 minutes at sustainable intensity (RPE 7–8 by final round). Fat loss follows total weekly energy expenditure — a 12-minute beginner EMOM done 4× weekly beats one 30-minute death session done once. Estimate burn with our Calories Calculator using MET values for circuit training (~8–10 METs).

EMOM vs every 2 minutes (E2MOM) — when to switch?

Use E2MOM when work sets exceed 40 seconds or involve heavy barbell complexes. Power cleans for 5 reps often need 45–55 seconds — an E2MOM structure preserves technique. Move to true EMOM when work fits in 25–35 seconds with load you can repeat for 10+ rounds.

Can I do EMOM every day?

Not sustainably. Two to three EMOM sessions per week with different movement patterns allows recovery. Daily EMOM with overlapping muscle groups (pull-ups + rowing + deadlifts) accumulates grip and spinal fatigue faster than running mileage alone.

Why do I score worse on the same EMOM after a running block?

Endurance-heavy training shifts muscle fiber recruitment toward efficiency, not peak power. Short-term EMOM output may drop 5–10% while 5K times improve. Retest after 1–2 weeks of reintroduced strength work — not after one fatigued session.
