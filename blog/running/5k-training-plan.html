8-Week 5K Training Plan

Updated July 2026 · 7 min read

Whether you're running your first 5K or chasing a new PR, eight weeks is enough time to build fitness without burning out. This plan balances easy aerobic running with one quality session per week and a gradual long run — the minimum effective dose for a fast 5K.

Who This Plan Is For

This plan suits runners who can already jog 20–30 minutes continuously. Beginners should add an extra easy week before starting; intermediate runners can use the optional speed sessions in weeks 5–8.

  • Beginners: Focus on completing every run. Walk breaks are fine on easy days.
  • Intermediate: Hit the prescribed paces using our Pace Calculator or Race Predictor to set goal splits.

Weekly Structure

Four runs per week keeps volume manageable while delivering enough stimulus to improve. The pattern repeats with progressive mileage:

  • Easy run — conversational pace, builds aerobic base
  • Quality session — intervals or tempo, develops speed and lactate tolerance
  • Easy run — recovery and volume
  • Long run — capped at 60 minutes; teaches pacing and mental toughness

Weeks 1–4: Build the Base

The first month prioritizes consistency over intensity. Your easy pace should feel genuinely easy — use the Zone 2 Calculator if you're unsure.

Week 1 Mon: Easy 25 min · Wed: 6 × 400m at 5K pace, 400m jog · Fri: Easy 25 min · Sun: Long 35 min Week 2 Mon: Easy 28 min · Wed: 5 × 800m at 5K pace, 400m jog · Fri: Easy 28 min · Sun: Long 40 min Week 3 Mon: Easy 30 min · Wed: Tempo 15 min at 10K pace · Fri: Easy 30 min · Sun: Long 45 min Week 4 (recovery) Mon: Easy 25 min · Wed: 4 × 400m at 5K pace · Fri: Easy 25 min · Sun: Long 35 min easy

Weeks 5–8: Sharpen for Race Day

Volume peaks in week 6, then tapers. Quality sessions get race-specific — shorter reps at goal 5K pace with full recovery.

Week 5 Mon: Easy 30 min · Wed: 3 × 1 mile at 5K pace, 3 min jog · Fri: Easy 30 min · Sun: Long 50 min Week 6 Mon: Easy 32 min · Wed: 5 × 1K at 5K pace, 2 min jog · Fri: Easy 30 min · Sun: Long 55 min Week 7 Mon: Easy 28 min · Wed: 3 × 800m at 5K pace · Fri: Easy 25 min · Sun: Long 40 min Week 8 (race week) Mon: Easy 20 min · Wed: 4 × 400m at 5K pace · Fri: Rest · Sun: Race day

Race-Day Pacing

Start 5–10 seconds per mile slower than goal pace for the first kilometer. Settle into rhythm by the halfway mark, then push the final mile. Check your goal splits with the Pace Chart before race morning.

Recovery and Progress

Two rest or cross-training days per week are built in. Sleep, hydration, and easy-day discipline matter as much as the hard sessions. If a workout feels impossible, swap it for an easy run — missing one quality day beats losing a week to injury.
