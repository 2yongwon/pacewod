10-Week 10K Training Plan

Updated July 2026 · 8 min read

The 10K sits in a sweet spot — long enough to demand aerobic fitness, short enough to require real speed. This 10-week plan assumes you can comfortably run 30–40 minutes and have completed a 5K or similar race. It adds volume, tempo work, and race-pace specificity on top of that base.

Prerequisites

Before starting, you should be running 15–20 miles per week with one weekly long run of at least 45 minutes. If you're coming off the couch, complete an 8-week 5K plan first.

  • Current weekly mileage: 15–25 miles
  • Long run baseline: 45–60 minutes
  • One recent 5K or time trial for pace setting

Use the Race Predictor to translate your 5K time into 10K goal paces.

Training Philosophy

10K fitness comes from three pillars: aerobic volume (easy runs), lactate threshold (tempo), and VO2 max (intervals). This plan hits all three while keeping easy days truly easy — roughly 80% of weekly mileage at conversational pace.

Phase 1: Base Building (Weeks 1–4)

Increase long run duration and introduce weekly tempo segments. Quality stays controlled — you're teaching your body to hold faster paces, not redlining every session.

Week 1

  • MonEasy 35 min
  • TueTempo 20 min at 10K pace
  • ThuEasy 35 min
  • SatLong 55 min

Week 2

  • MonEasy 38 min
  • Tue5 × 1K at 5K pace, 2 min jog
  • ThuEasy 38 min
  • SatLong 60 min

Week 3

  • MonEasy 40 min
  • TueTempo 25 min at 10K pace
  • ThuEasy 40 min
  • SatLong 65 min

Week 4 (recovery)

  • MonEasy 35 min
  • Tue4 × 800m at 5K pace
  • ThuEasy 35 min
  • SatLong 50 min easy

Phase 2: Race Specific (Weeks 5–8)

Long runs include 10K-pace segments. Tempo runs extend to 30 minutes. Intervals shift to mile repeats at 10K effort — the pace you'll hold on race day.

Week 5

  • MonEasy 40 min
  • Tue3 × 1 mile at 10K pace, 3 min jog
  • ThuEasy 40 min
  • SatLong 70 min (last 15 min at 10K pace)

Week 6

  • MonEasy 42 min
  • TueTempo 30 min at 10K pace
  • ThuEasy 42 min
  • SatLong 75 min

Week 7

  • MonEasy 40 min
  • Tue4 × 1K at 10K pace, 90 sec jog
  • ThuEasy 38 min
  • SatLong 65 min (last 20 min at 10K pace)

Week 8

  • MonEasy 38 min
  • Tue3 × 1 mile at 10K pace
  • ThuEasy 35 min
  • SatLong 55 min easy

Phase 3: Taper (Weeks 9–10)

Cut volume by 30–40% while keeping legs sharp with short race-pace bursts. Trust the training — fitness is banked; now you recover.

Week 9

  • MonEasy 30 min
  • TueTempo 15 min at 10K pace
  • ThuEasy 28 min
  • SatLong 45 min

Week 10 (race week)

  • MonEasy 25 min
  • Wed4 × 400m at 10K pace
  • FriRest
  • Sun10K race

10K Race Strategy

Run even splits. The first 2K should feel controlled; kilometers 3–7 are where the race is won. Use the Pace Calculator to memorize your per-kilometer splits before the start line.

Staying Healthy

Add one full rest day and optional cross-training (cycling, swimming) on non-running days. If weekly mileage jumps feel aggressive, repeat week 3 before moving to week 4. Consistency beats hero weeks.
